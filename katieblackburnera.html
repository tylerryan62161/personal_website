Katie Blackburn

Katie Blackburn is the daughter of Mike Brown and granddaughter of Paul Brown. She is anticipated to become the next official owner of the Cincinnati Bengals once her father either steps down or passes away. She joined the team's front office in 1991, however, she has slowly but surely taken on more responsibility within the organization starting back in 2009. The results have been mixed thus far so we'll explore the impact she's already had and how that may project to the future.

A Promising Future

Just like her father, Katie cares deeply about the performance of the team. Also like Mike, she wants to make her father and fellow family members proud. Unlike her father, Katie doesn't desire to be the dictator of the team. In fact, she relishes cooperating with other intelligent, successful decision makers to leverage their skills and form a consensus around the best decision for the team. This alone would be a significant departure from the Mike Brown era but she's also prepared to open the team's checkbook in pursuit of making improvements as well as cut ties with underperforming members. These factors lead me, and hopefully others, to believe that while the team is currently rebuilding, the future is bright.

  • First female chief contract negotiator in the NFL
  • On track to be the 5th female president in the NFL
  • Consensus based decision making rather than thinking she always knows best
  • Member of Workplace Diversity Committee
  • Chair of Super Bowl Advisory Committee
  • Most successful five year stretch in franchise history immediately after she was given control
  • Willingness to move on from underperforming coaches/players
  • Revamped social media presence
  • Finally updating jerseys after two decades
  • Celebrating greatest past members - hinting at ring of honor
  • Lets head coach assume full control over coaching staff and players
  • Willing to spend on ancillary aspects of the team
  • Willingness to spend in free agency

After identifying these factors, the team appears to be in much better hands as we move forward. Here are some parting words from Katie herself:

“I take a lot of pride in the fact I come from a football family and the history of my grandfather in coaching, his experience in Cleveland. I see how much that means to my dad and how affected by it he was and really the way I look at it is sort of something I’m proud of and therefore take it probably more personally than I would if I were in another business because there is that tie you have. You want to live up to the history of it and keep it going.”

Father's Footsteps

Katie was born in Cleveland, Ohio in 1965. This was shortly after her grandfather, Paul Brown, was fired from the Cleveland Browns and during the period of time her father, Mike Brown, was practicing law in Cleveland. Just like her father, she went to Dartmouth College and decided to practice law initially in her career. She received her law degree from the University of Cincinnati College of Law and worked at a Cincinnati law firm. That is, until her grandfather passed away and her father needed help running the team. She joined the Cincinnati Bengals after Paul's passing in 1991.

Trail Blazer

As you might imagine, the NFL has mostly been a boy's club, even on the business side of things. Katie has been a pioneer in this regard. When in highschool, she would sit in on contract negotiations with her father and gradfather and made calls to player agents while in law school. This experience led to her being the first female chief contract negotiator in the league regarding player contracts. She handled the team's business with TV and radio networks as well as led the team's outreach to the community. She took on all of these responsibilities by the year 2000. She is currently the team's Executive Vice President, second in command to her father, and projects to become only the fifth female owner/President in the league.

Transition of Power

Beginning in 2009, Mike began transitioning more and more responsibilities from himself to his daughter, Katie. Marvin Lewis, the head coach at the time, is often given partial credit for this transition, saying he negotiated a more modern approach when discussing a contract extension with Mike. Katie was more inclined to make decisions with a team consensus than to act as dictator over every aspect of the team. Her own mother, Nancy Brown, had this to say:

“Katie's more open to consensus, where Mike is more self-directed.”

The results speak for themselves. The team would go on to have its most successful period in team history, reaching the playoffs every year from 2011-2015. Beyond team responsibilities, Katie has been appointed to significant positions by the league office. These include the Workplace Diversity Committee and she chairs the Super Bowl Advisory Committee.

“She’s incredibly well respected. She's been impactful. She’s somebody who brings not only vast business knowledge but tremendous football knowledge as well. She’s incredibly insightful for what she brings to the committee.” - Robert Gulliver, NFL VP

The Workplace Diversity Committee position can be seen as a nod to her grandfather's role in breaking the race barrier.

Positive Steps

Some of the signs of the new direction of the team have been more obvious than others. One key decision was moving on from head coach Marvin Lewis. Lewis is a good man, a good coach, and experienced moderate success. However, 16 years without a single playoff win is simply intolerable. Mike wanted to exercise his famous loyalty to underperforming coaches and retain Lewis even further. Katie saw the writing on the wall and ripped off the bandaid. Now, the team has experienced two losing seasons since Lewis' departure, but rebuilding is a natural part of every franchise as the transition to a younger and more talented core of players takes time. Just how much time exactly? We'll see how Katie handles it, but five straight years of playoffs immediately following her ascension to power should have bought enough goodwill with fans to wait and see how her first rebuild progresses. Other signs include the revamping of the team's social media presence, updating the jerseys after almost two decades, and a 50 year celebration of the great players in team history. Fans have been clamoring for an official ring of honor for great players and coaches, and the 50 year celebration was a good step while the team hints at the potential ring of honor being instituted. As for more directly performance related issues, Katie let her handpicked head coach hire the largest coaching staff in team history as well as install tens of millions of dollars in upgrades to team facilities as he saw fit. This goes a long way to shaking off the "cheap" reputation of the team. Finally, and possibly the most exciting change, has been the team's willingness to spend big on free agents, bucking the team's cheap reputation here as well. The team continues to fan the flames of excitement over the upcoming free agency this year.
